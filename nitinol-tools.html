Explainer 03

Crystal transformation

Austenite is a rigid cubic B2 lattice. Cool it, and the lattice shears into twinned monoclinic martensite. Some alloys pass through a third, low-hysteresis R-phase on the way.

Explainer 04

Slip vs twinning

In ordinary metals, strain permanently slips whole atomic planes past each other. In nitinol's martensite, strain instead rotates bonds — no bonds break, and heating restores the original shape.

0.00
Slip (permanent — bonds break)
When strain exceeds the critical value, an atomic plane shifts by one spacing. Releasing doesn't undo it — that's plastic deformation.
Twinning (reversible — bonds rotate)
The lattice shears; atoms move by partial spacings without breaking bonds. Releasing returns everything — that's the shape-memory effect.

About these tools

First-order pedagogical models — derived from Otsuka & Ren (2005), Brinson (1993) / Lagoudas (2008) constitutive frameworks, and the empirical numbers in Nitinol in Plain Language and Introduction to Nitinol Actuators.

For production design, use ASTM F2004 / F2082 / F2516 / F3142 tests and a calibrated constitutive model. See SMA-REACT for open-source Lagoudas calibration.
